Lacomida

UX Design / UI Design / Brand Strategy 

Food Delivery App for A Restaurant in Louisiana

Lacomida is a local restaurant located in New Orleans Louisiana. Lacomida strives to deliver healthy meals and entrees. Lacomida target customers like commuters, busy workers and student who lack the time or ability to prepare food.

Duration

April 2021 to July 2021

The Problem

Busy workers, commuters and busy students lack the time to prepare food.

The Goal

Design an app for Lacomida that allows users to quickly order healthy meals for delivery or pick up.

My Role

UX designer, designing an app for Lacomida from conception to delivery.

Responsibilities

Conduct interviews, wireframing, prototyping, usability studies, accessibiliy consideration & Design

Understanding The Users

User Research: Summary

I conducted interviews and created empathy maps to understand the users I’m designing for and their needs. A primary user group identified through research was working adults who don’t have time to cook meals. 
This user group confirmed initial assumptions about Lacomida customers, but research also revealed that time was not the only factor limiting users from cooking at home. Other users problems included obligations, interests or challenges that make it difficult to get groceries for cooking or going to restaurant in person.

Users Pain Point

Time

Working adults and busy students do not have enough time to prepare meals.

Accessibility

Platforms for ordering  food are not equipped with assistive technology.

Featur es

Platforms for ordering food do not have some of the features that users need to order healthy meal quickly.

Persona: Erica Smith

 

User Journey Map

 

Mapping Erica’s user journey revealed how helpful it would be for users to have access to a dedicated Lacomida app.

Starting The Design

Paper Wireframes 

Taking the time to draft iterations of each screen of the app on paper ensured that the elements that made it to digital wireframes would be well-suited to address users pain points. For the home screen, I prioritised a quick and easy ordering process to help users save time.

Stars were used to mark the elements of sketch that would be used in the initial wireframe

Digital Wireframe 

As initial design face continued, I made sure to base screen designs on feedback and findings from the user research.  Easy Navigation was a key user need to address in the designs in addition to equipping the app to work with assistive technologies.

Low Fidelity Prototype

The low fidelity prototype connect the primary user flow of  ordering food, so the prototype could be used in a usability study with users. 

View Lacomida App Low-fidelity Prototype

Usability Study Findings

I conducted two rounds  of usability studies. Findings from the first study helped guide the designs from wireframes to mockups. The second study used a high-fidelity prototype and revealed what aspects of the mockups needed refining.

Round 1 findings
  1. Users want to add orders quickly
  2. Users want more information about items
  3. Users want to customize order
Rounding 2 findings
  1. Pickup or delivery option should be the first and not the last step in the ordering process

Refining The Design

Changes After  Usability 1

Early designs allowed users to add item to cart in on click, but after usability study I added additional page to see more information about food and also customize order.

Changes After Usability 2

The second usability study reveal frustration with getting to chose pick up or delivery option at checkout, to fix this I made the pickup up or delivery option the first step in the flow by adding the pick up or delivery option on the homepage

Going Foward

Takeaways

Impact

The app made users feel like Lacomida really think about how to make them happy. 

One quote from peer feedback: 
“The made it so easy to order, customize and reorder food with a lot of payment options, I would definitely use this app to order healthy meals.” 

What i have learned

While designing Lacomida app, I learned usability study and peer feedback are keys to a user centred design.

Next Steps

  1. Conduct another round of usability studies to validate whether the pain points users experienced have been effectively addressed. 
  2. Audit competitors frequently to stay updated on areas to improve in designs. 
  3. Conduct more user research to determine any new areas of need.
